About Bengal Fonts

As the Lingua Franca of over 200 Million people worldwide, Bengali Alphabet is widely used to express feelings and emotion. From the pages of books to movie posters, Bengali alphabet is used in various colors and formations. Illustrators, Artists and Designers use different formations of the alphabet keeping the basic Bengali Scripture intact. Despite being one of the top ten languages in the world, there are a very limited number of aesthetically pleasing and impressive font in Bengali in comparison to other traditional languages. For this reason, it is very difficult for the designers to bring variation, innovation and creativity in communication and publishing industry. To solve the issue and from the obligation to our language, we present to you- The Bengal Font Foundry. Apart from publishing new Bengali fonts from time to time, our goal is to inspire people to design new Bengali Font.

VISION

People argue that, despite everything, it is the content - what you actually write that is important - not how it looks. One can say that choosing a font is like dressing the right way depending on the impression you want you make. Regardless of how good a text you have, people will nevertheless, consciously or unconsciously, also judge the content by how it looks. The choice of font can determine if your text communicates with authority or honesty. Perhaps it calls out to, whispers or even flirts with the recipient. In other words, the choice of typeface can be most crucial when it comes to how your communication is perceived. For example, political election campaigns are very careful about what they choose to communicate their message with. You will probably never see campaign posters written in soft script type. It would be more suitable for a wine label than trying to get a strong political statement across. In the process of creating a brand, a great deal of work is invested in values and the core of the company. The visual tools then need to be in harmony with the values and keywords arrived at. Different fonts express different things. If you want to appear modern, classic, more personal, perhaps a bit youthful, more mature, traditional ... all these keywords will require a different choice of typeface. In addition, the typeface chosen will play a part in creating recognition, so it is important that a brand is loyal to only using its chosen typeface in all communication. If, for example, Apple were to suddenly launch a campaign using historical Gothic letterforms, people would simply fail to recognise them. Big companies often design their own typeface to ensure that they have a unique font that no other competitor can use it and to reinforce their identity in the market.


MISSION

Since the start of advertising, businesses have learned that design choices matter. The way you present your ad, even down to the font you use, makes an initial impression on your customers. That means that you need to pay careful attention to every detail of your ad. The type of font you use not only serves to catch your potential customers’ attention, but also to send a message of what your business is all about. This includes your mission statement and the overall “feeling” that you convey. In general, typography is seen as more professional than handwriting fonts. However, there are times when handwriting can be an appropriate choice. Handwriting fonts are unique and stand out from the crowd, which means they can be a good choice for getting your marketing materials noticed.
